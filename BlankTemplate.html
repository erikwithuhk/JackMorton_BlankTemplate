Are you as fit as the fittest men and women in the world…for 60 seconds?
What?
The Athlete Challenge pitted you against one of Reebok's sponsored elite CrossFit athletes in an "as many reps as possible" one-on-one virtual battle for 60 seconds.
Participants chose a specific athlete they wanted to challenge, and faced them head-to-head for a full minute, hoping to keep pace.
The dramatic final 10 seconds of each participant's attempt were captured, then composited with the elite athlete's video and turned into a shareable video that was created on the spot as a takeaway.
When & where?
Starting in early March 2015, the “Reebok Athlete Challenge” was deployed at every CrossFit Games regional event plus the finals in Carson, CA—9 cities around the world in total.
How?
The challenge was driven by a Mac Mini running software Genuine developed leveraging openFrameworks, a C++ creative coding toolkit, and used a GoPro Hero4 connected to a BlackMagic UltraStudio Mini Recorder to record the live action. The mini recorder is a video capture device for both HDMI and 3G-SDI sources which connects to a Thunderbolt port on the Mac Mini.

Genuine also created a secondary app (using Apple's native AVFoundation framework), which had two functions: first, it composited the participant's capture with the last 10 seconds of the elite athlete's video, creating a side-by-side view of the dramatic finish. Reebok and CrossFit-branded titles along with the event date/location were added to the 15-second, 640x480 resolution video, suitable for sharing on social channels.

The app's second job was to upload that final video to a custom CMS, which sent out an email to the participant, leading them to a microsite where they could download or share their video. The entire video creation process, including uploading and sending out the email, took less than a minute on average.
Hardware:
  • Apple Mac Mini 3.8GHz
  • 80" vertically mounted screens
  • GoPro Hero4
  • BlackMagic UltraStudio Mini Recorder
  • Logitech Bluetooth keyboard
  • Pelican Case(s)
 Software:
  • Custom software developed by Genuine
  • openFrameworks
  • Apple AVFoundation
